Mastering the ATS: How to Beat the Bots

Is your CV getting lost in the digital void? Learn how Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) work and key strategies to ensure your application actually reaches a human recruiter.

ATS software scans CVs for specific keywords related to the job description. If your CV doesn't contain these keywords in the right context, it might be automatically rejected.

Key Takeaways:

  • Use standard headings (e.g., "Work Experience," not "My Journey").
  • Avoid complex tables or graphics that bots can't parse.
  • Tailor keywords for every single application.

The One-Page Myth: How Long Should Your CV Really Be?

The debate rages on. Should you squeeze a decade of experience onto a single page, or is a two-page CV acceptable? We break down the rules based on career stage.

The "one-page rule" is largely outdated for anyone other than entry-level candidates. Recruiters prefer a readable two-page document over a cramped one-pager with tiny font.

  • Entry-Level (0-3 years): Stick to one page. Focus on education and internships.
  • Mid-Career (3-10 years): Two pages is standard and often expected.
  • Senior Level (10+ years): Two pages is ideal; three is acceptable only for academic or highly technical CVs requiring extensive publication lists.
